A Breakdown of ASOS AI Personalization Strategy
Here are six ways in which ASOS uses AI for personalization to improve customer experience and conversion rates.
1. AI-powered recommender system and collaborative filtering
ASOS implements machine learning to identify products that can be recommended to customers, based on customers’ browsing patterns. For instance, if a few different customers buy two products together (Eg., a red dress and black belt), the system then recommends these two products to other customers who have similar demographic profiles.
ASOS’s recommendation system and collaborative filtering feature processes vast amounts of browser and customer data continuously, as new products are added to the website every week.
A recent study by IBM reveals that three out of five customers would like to use AI features when shopping. ASOS’s AI system is constantly working to identify and understand individual preferences to meet the increasing need for personalization in eCommerce.
ASOS AI systems work with thousands of products as the brand offers clothes, accessories, and footwear from multiple brands under one roof. The brand has product SKUs and data that only AI can handle, as it is impossible to sift through so many products manually.
Here are the benefits of ASOS AI personalization:
-
Offer personalized experience
-
Provides recommendations based on individual preference
-
Reduces browsing through products for customers
-
Enhances engagement rate, conversions, and customer satisfaction
-
Increases AOV through recommendations
2. Advanced fit assistant tool
One of the biggest issues in eCommerce fashion is sizing accuracy. It is also a reason for returns and exchanges, making shopping more complex. ASOS tackles this problem with a Fit Assistant Tool, powered by machine learning.
The tool provides personalized size recommendations based on various data points. For instance, for existing customers, the system analyzes data from past purchases and returns to understand customer sizes. For new customers, it asks a couple of quick questions about height, weight, and fit preference to receive accurate recommendations.
The Fit Assistant technology combines garment information with purchase histories across ASOS brands, helping customers to find the right size. The tool displays the suggested size along with the percentage of other shoppers who have purchased the size.
Here’s how having the Fit Assistant tool helps ASOS AI personalization strategy:
-
Reduces size-related returns and exchanges
-
Increased conversion rates
-
Increased customer satisfaction levels
-
Helps customers make more informed choices
3. ASOS AI personalization with OpenAI-powered stylist
ASOS incorporated Azure OpenAI Service-powered AI Stylist to make it easier for customers to discover new looks. The AI Stylist tool is different from traditional search or filtering features. The conversational tool interacts with customers to understand their style preferences, occasions they’re shopping for, and specific requirements.
The AI system also collects data about fashion trends from other sources, insights from ASOS designers, and customers personal style preference to recommend products. This shifts the passive browsing experience to interactive discovery.
Here’s how the AI Stylist tool helps ASOS:

Provides instant and personalized style suggestions

Enhances product discovery for customers

Makes shopping interactive and engaging

Enhances experience for young customers.

Provides modern, conversational shopping experiences
4. AI-powered supply chain mapping and traceability
Transparency, sustainability, and ethical practices are important factors for businesses. ASOS partnered with TrusTrace to implement AI-based supply chain traceability. The tool provides real-time visibility into its supply chain.
The AI platform collects and validates supply chain data, streamlines risks, manages regulatory compliance, footprint calculations, etc. The tool helps the brand to understand and address supply chain risks before they occur.
The tool also helps centralize documentation, manage compliance standards, and integrate updated information with the system. With this tool, ASOS aims to have 100% of its own products’ supply chains mapped to raw material level by 2030.
Here’s how the tool helps ASOS:
-
Provides real-time visibility across the supply chain
-
Proactive risk management and compliance
-
Supports sustainability goals
-
Build trust with customers
-
Enables ethical and transparent fashion
-
Creates resilience in supply chain operations
5. Customer-product interaction analysis for enhanced personalization
A study shows that shoppers complete purchases 47% faster with AI support. ASOS enhances shopping speed with AI. Its AI systems focus on how customers interact with products throughout their shopping journey.
The brand uses AI and ML to understand customer interactions and improve personalization at every touchpoint. For instance, the brand uses a Profile Builder feature that captures information from customers about their style preference.
So, how does Profile Builder work? The system selects a sample of 36 products from various product categories and presents one product per category to learn the most about customer preferences.
The tool then uses these insights to personalize customer experiences via recommendations, email marketing, promotional messages, etc. Features such as “Your Edit”, “Style Match”, and “You Might Also Like” are displayed throughout customers’ shopping journey on the ASOS website.

Enhances customer engagement throughout the shopping journey

The brand uses Power Automate for process automation, and Teams Premium for automated meeting summaries, notes, actions, and live translations. ASOS uses AI-based tools to increase efficiency, decision-making, and rapid prototyping of new customer experiences.
