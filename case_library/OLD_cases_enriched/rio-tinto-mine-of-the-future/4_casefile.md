# Case File: Rio Tinto's Mine of the Future

## Executive Summary

Rio Tinto has developed what it calls "intelligent mines" that integrate autonomous vehicles, digital twins, machine learning, and data analytics to optimize mining operations. The company's Gudai-Darri mine represents their most technologically advanced facility, featuring autonomous haul trucks, drills, and water carts, along with robotic systems for ore sampling and parts distribution. The mine operates with remote monitoring from Perth, 1,500 km away, and incorporates a digital twin of the processing plant for real-time optimization.

The company's broader "Mine of the Future" program focuses on creating integrated, data-driven operations where all assets are networked and capable of making autonomous decisions. This approach has already demonstrated measurable value, with integrated planning systems extracting US$10 million in additional value through better market responsiveness and operational optimization. The technology deployment aims to make mining operations safer and more productive through the combination of automation, analytics, and machine learning.

## Detailed Findings

**C1 - Mine Capacity Specifications (REJECTED)**

- **Evidence**: "With an expected life of more than 40 years and an annual capacity of 43 million tons, Gudai-Darri will underpin future production of Rio Tinto's Pilbara Blend product."
- **Source**: S2
- **Issue**: Basic operational specifications, not a capability improvement claim
- **Recommendation**: Focus on technology-driven efficiency or productivity improvements rather than baseline capacity metrics

**C2 - Production Volume Increases (REJECTED)**

- **Recommendation**: Need evidence that increases are due to AI/automation optimization rather than new facility capacity
