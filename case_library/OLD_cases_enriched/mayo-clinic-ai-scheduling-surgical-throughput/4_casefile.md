# Mayo Clinic AI Scheduling and Surgical Throughput Case File

## Executive Summary

Mayo Clinic has implemented comprehensive AI solutions to optimize surgical scheduling and throughput, particularly in cardiac surgery operations. Through partnership with Opmed.ai, the health system has deployed AI models that significantly improve case duration predictions, reduce operating room waste, and increase surgical capacity. The initiative demonstrates measurable improvements in operational efficiency, with AI predictions reducing scheduling errors from 60 minutes to 34 minutes per case and saving over 200 operating room hours annually per room.

Beyond surgical scheduling, Mayo Clinic has established itself as a leader in healthcare AI adoption through an enablement-focused approach that empowers clinicians to develop AI applications. The organization has successfully deployed AI across multiple domains, including FDA-cleared cardiac diagnostic algorithms and capacity management systems that proved critical during the COVID-19 pandemic.

**Claim**: AI model significantly improved prediction accuracy, reducing Mean Absolute Error from 60 minutes to 34 minutes per case

**Evidence**: Opmed.ai's AI model for cardiac surgery case duration prediction demonstrated substantial improvement over traditional estimation methods in controlled testing at Mayo Clinic.

**Supporting Quote**: "In controlled tests, our AI model significantly surpassed traditional estimation methods, reducing the Mean Absolute Error (MAE) from 60 minutes per case to just 34 minutes."
