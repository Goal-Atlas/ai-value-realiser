# Case File: Relativity Space's 3D-Printed Rocket Manufacturing

## Executive Summary

Relativity Space has pioneered a transformational approach to rocket manufacturing through additive manufacturing (3D printing), fundamentally reimagining how rockets are designed, built, and deployed. The company's Terran 1 rocket is 85% 3D-printed, representing the world's first orbital-class rocket built almost entirely through additive manufacturing. This approach reduces part count from thousands to under 1,000 components, with some engines containing just 3 parts compared to traditional engines with 3,000 parts.

The transformation has generated significant commercial validation, with $1.65 billion in launch contracts secured before the company's first launch attempt. Relativity's manufacturing approach enables rockets to be built from scratch to space in 60 days, dramatically compressing traditional multi-year development cycles. The March 2023 Terran 1 test flight successfully demonstrated that 3D-printed materials could withstand the intense atmospheric pressures during launch, validating the technical feasibility of this manufacturing transformation.

Beyond cost and speed advantages, this approach democratizes space access by significantly decreasing costs and enabling new customer segments like college students to send experiments to space. The company's ultimate vision extends to printing rocket components on the Moon and Mars, eliminating the need to transport parts from Earth for future missions.
