# Case File: Komatsu's Autonomous Haulage System for Mining Operations

## Executive Summary

Komatsu has established itself as a leader in autonomous mining operations through its FrontRunner Autonomous Haulage System (AHS), which enables unmanned operation of ultra-class mining trucks. Since the world's first commercial AHS deployment in 2008 at CODELCO's Gabriela Mistral copper mine in Chile, Komatsu has achieved significant scale with over 700 autonomous trucks operating worldwide as of February 2024.

The system has demonstrated substantial operational impact, with AHS trucks hauling 7.5 billion metric tons of materials by February 2024, building on the earlier milestone of moving over 4 billion tonnes since initial deployment. Komatsu has deployed AHS at 23 mine sites across five countries, serving the copper, iron ore, and oil sands industries. The technology represents a significant advancement in mining automation, combining Komatsu's ultra-class dump trucks with proven fleet management systems to deliver improved safety, productivity, and cost optimization for mining operations.
