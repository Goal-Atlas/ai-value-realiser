# Texas Rangers Databricks Player Performance Analysis Case File

## Executive Summary

The Texas Rangers successfully leveraged Databricks' lakehouse platform to transform their data analytics capabilities, ultimately contributing to their 2023 World Series championship. Facing overwhelming data volumes from biomechanical sensors and high-speed cameras that their existing infrastructure could not handle, the Rangers migrated to Databricks in 2021. This transformation delivered exceptional business value with a 375% ROI, 4x cost improvement over their previous cloud data warehouse, and 61% productivity gains across data teams. The platform enabled real-time analytics that informed critical roster construction decisions and strategic gameplay choices throughout their championship season.

## Detailed Findings

### Data Infrastructure Transformation

**Claim C6: Infrastructure Limitations by 2021**
- **Status**: APPROVED
- **Finding**: The Rangers' existing Microsoft and Oracle on-premises data management tools became insufficient by 2021 due to explosive growth in biomechanical data volume from motion capture systems and high-speed cameras generating thousands of images per second.

### Cost and Performance Benefits

**Claim C7: 375% ROI Achievement**
- **Status**: APPROVED
- **Finding**: Independent analysis confirmed the Rangers achieved a 375% return on investment from their Databricks deployment, demonstrating significant business value from the platform migration.

**Claim C8: 4x Cost-Effectiveness Improvement**
- **Status**: APPROVED
- **Finding**: The Rangers realized 4x improved cost-effectiveness compared to their prior cloud-based data warehouse, indicating substantial operational savings.

**Claim C9: 10x Data Ingestion Cost Improvement**
- **Status**: APPROVED
- **Finding**: Data ingestion processes became 10x more cost-effective under the Databricks platform, addressing one of the Rangers' primary infrastructure challenges.

**Claim C10: 61% Productivity Improvement**
- **Status**: APPROVED
- **Finding**: The Rangers' data teams experienced a 61% productivity improvement, enabling more efficient analysis and faster insights generation.

### Operational Capabilities

**Claim C1: Centralized Data Management**
- **Status**: APPROVED
- **Finding**: Databricks eliminated the Rangers' previous patchwork of software systems that created redundancy, data silos, and processing delays, providing unified data management regardless of structure.

**Claim C2: Real-Time Decision Making**
- **Status**: APPROVED
- **Finding**: The platform's real-time insights capabilities empowered coaches and players to make informed decisions during practice sessions and games, providing immediate competitive advantages.

**Claim C3: Elimination of Processing Delays**
- **Status**: APPROVED
- **Finding**: Databricks Structured Streaming eliminated previous delays of hours or days in data processing, enabling timely insights that could impact game outcomes and injury prevention.

**Claim C11: Enhanced Data Availability**
- **Status**: APPROVED
- **Finding**: The Rangers achieved 2x more data availability at lower latency, fostering improved collaboration among analysts, players, and coaches while enabling faster ad-hoc analysis iteration.

### Strategic Impact

**Claim C4: Roster Construction Analytics**
- **Status**: APPROVED
- **Finding**: Databricks' lakehouse platform directly powered analytics that informed General Manager Chris Young's roster construction decisions, contributing to the team's championship success.

**Claim C5: Championship Season Analytics**
- **Status**: APPROVED
- **Finding**: The platform fueled analytics that informed strategic decisions made by players, manager Bruce Bochy, and coaching staff throughout all 162 regular season and 17 postseason games leading to their first championship.
